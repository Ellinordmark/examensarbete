The underlying research

eCOmpass is the result of research by Kenneth Carling, Vijay Paidi, and Niklas Rudholm at Dalarna University and the Institute of Retail Economics, who explored how smarter location planning for stores and services can reduce CO₂ emissions

Their studies showed that placing facilities in the right spots can significantly cut the distance people need to travel, lowering overall emissions. Using real travel data and optimization methods, the researchers developed a tool that helps compare existing and potential locations.

They tested their approach with real-worldcases, including the growth of IKEA’s store network in Sweden, and found that strategic planning can make a big environmental difference.

If you want to read more about the research, you can download the working paper here.
